FAQs

The National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS) is an educational organisation which imparts education through Open and Distance Learning Mode and also conducts examinations for grant of certificates up to pre-degree level at par with other National Boards viz. the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) and Council for the Indian School Certificate Examination (CISCE).

Open and Distance Learning (ODL) is a flexible education system that allows students to learn at their own pace, often from home, using study materials and online resources, without attending regular classes.

The National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS) is a national board of education in India, established by the Ministry of Education. It provides education through open and distance learning, offering flexibility to learners who cannot attend regular schools due to various reasons.

NIOS offers a range of courses:
  • Open Basic Education (OBE) for levels equivalent to classes III, V, and VIII
  • Secondary Course (equivalent to class X)
  • Senior Secondary Course (equivalent to class XII)
  • Vocational Education Courses
  • Life Enrichment Programs

Yes, certificates issued by NIOS for Secondary and Senior Secondary levels are recognized at par with those from other national boards like CBSE and CISCE. They are valid for higher education and government jobs.

  • For Secondary (Class X): Anyone who has passed class VIII and is at least 14 years old, or provides a self-certificate stating they can pursue the course.
  • For Senior Secondary (Class XII): Students must have passed the Secondary (Class X) examination from a recognized board and meet the minimum age requirement.

Admissions are 100% online. You can:
  • Register directly on the NIOS website
  • Visit a nearby Accredited Institution (Study Centre) for help
  • Go to a Regional Centre
  • Use Common Services Centres (CSC) across India for assistance
